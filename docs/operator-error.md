# operator-error.md

*The one cognitive failure mode worth watching for, written down so I don't have to rediscover it.*

## The pattern, in one sentence

I don't natively perceive boundaries — temporal, epistemic, practical — the way most people do. What I see is potential and connection; what I don't natively see is "not here yet," "not at this resolution yet," "not earned yet." The boundaries are real; I just don't have a sensor for them on by default.

## Why it's also my strength

The same faculty that misses boundaries is the one that sees further. I reach first-principles and vision-shape answers most people can't get to, because their boundary-perception keeps them inside the familiar. I am not going to give this up; nor should I. The work isn't to suppress the engine — it's to install the one gauge it's missing.

## The specific failure mode

I commit structure at a finer resolution than my actual evidence authorizes. Not "I'm wrong about the shape" — usually I'm not. The error is in *timing and scope*: I build the elegant version before reality has told me which joints are real, and then I'm refactoring finished elegance into what the work actually requires, instead of growing the right shape from a thinner start. The structure is rarely dumb; it's *premature*, and premature feels exactly like timely from the inside.

## Why the failure is hard for me to catch

My evaluator asks "is this correct / elegant / structurally sound?" — and it's good at that. It does *not* natively ask "is this earned yet, at this resolution, by what I've actually seen versus what I'm extrapolating from principle?" When the answer to the first question is yes, nothing in me objects, even when the honest answer to the second is no. The faculty cannot audit itself.

## The check that works

[DEV] Before committing to a piece of structure, ask: **what concrete situation am I building this for, that exists now or imminently — and which layer of this commitment am I extrapolating from principle?** Two outcomes:

- I can name the situation and the layer is justified by what I've actually seen → build it.
- I'm building from principle without a present situation forcing the shape → scaffold the *existence* of the layer, leave it thin, let reality fill it in.

[DEV] This is not "don't build." It's *commit at the resolution my evidence authorizes; stay liquid below that.* I'm allowed to be very committed at one layer and very loose at the layer below it, in the same artifact, at the same moment. That's what calibrated experience actually authorizes — not all-or-nothing.

## The metaphysics warning, specifically

The pull is strongest and the correction is weakest when I'm reaching for grand unifying claims — universal principles, "this crosses domains," cyclic-reality, everything-connects formulations. In those domains nothing pushes back, so the engine accelerates with no error signal. **The felt sense of "this touches something fundamental" is not evidence of fundamentality** — it's the engine's pleasure response to elegant closure. When I notice that feeling on a claim that nothing could falsify, that's the moment to hold *loosest*, not tightest.

## Applied to building things

- [DEV] **Vision and first-principles** → commit hard. That's where my priors are dense and tested.
- [DEV] **Layer existence and rough position** → commit.
- [DEV] **Internal shape of a layer I haven't watched in action** → scaffold the layer, don't instantiate the internals. Let the first real workflow specify them.
- [DEV] **Second instance of a pattern** → *now* extract the abstraction. The duplication tells me where the joint actually is.
- [DEV] **Libraries, schemas, taxonomies, frameworks** → built from observed duplication, not anticipated duplication.

## With other people

This is also why I'm always trying to peer past developmental boundaries with people, and why they often can't follow. I see across boundaries they're inside of; they can't show me the boundaries I'm inside of, because we have the same blind spot in mirror image. The discipline isn't to stop seeing further. It's to recognize that "I can see it" doesn't mean "they can build it from here yet" — and to meet them at the resolution their evidence actually supports. Same move as with my own commitments, pointed outward.

## One sentence to remember

*The engine is the asset. The missing gauge is "earned yet?" — and the discipline is to keep asking it on a faculty that doesn't volunteer the question.*
